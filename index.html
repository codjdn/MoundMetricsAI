      What this is, and isn't. The AI estimates joint positions from a single camera angle on the frames Auto-detect picks, and the app compares the resulting angles and ratios to general ranges seen in pitching biomechanics research. It's a rough 2D estimate, not a 3D motion-capture measurement, and the AI can misplace joints in a side view — always sanity-check the dots against the video, and run Auto-detect again if they look off.

      Everyone's mechanics vary with body type, mobility, and pitch type — landing outside a "typical range" here isn't a diagnosis of a problem, and landing inside one isn't a guarantee of safety or velocity. Treat these as talking points.

      For anything related to injury risk in particular — especially elbow and shoulder stress — a pitching coach, athletic trainer, or sports medicine / biomechanics lab can give a far more precise and individualized assessment than a single video ever can.
